After spending 2 months in Koh Lanta, I decided to move to the Philippines for 1 month to discover this amazing country.

![Coconut El Nido](/assets/img/coconut-el-nido.jpg)

The Philippines is an island country composed of more than 7 000 islands. It‚Äôs is known for its incredible crystal clear water, blue lagoon, and white sand beaches.

As a digital nomad, it‚Äôs not always the most recommended destination because the internet speed can vary a lot due to the geography of the country.

I‚Äôll explain in this article how I organized myself to have a good balance between work and staying in paradise location.

Here is the tour I did in the Philippines. I spent most of my time in Cebu City (I'll explain why just later), and just did 2 weekends on paradise places.

![My tour in the Philippines](/assets/img/2019-philippines/philippines-map.jpg)

## A little word on my digital nomad philosophy

Being digital nomad is **totally different** from being a traveler. 

I‚Äôm not in holidays. I‚Äôm not looking for moving every 2 days, eating at the airport and waking up at 3 AM to catch a boat. (I do like that, but only when I‚Äôm in holidays with my friends, it‚Äôs not sustainable on a daily basis).

When I‚Äôm in my daily life, I‚Äôm looking to find as much stability as possible so I can build a routine and be productive at work.

To achieve that, I stay in general between 2 and 4 months in a country. 

This time was a little different because I planned a long time ago to see a friend in India in April, so I decided to stay only 1 month in the Philippines.

Still, in one month I managed to find my routine.

## First, a few days in Manila

I landed first in Manila and worked 2 days from there. Manila was quite crowded, but I managed to be productive.

I first worked in an internet cafe full of people playing fortnite (interesting atmosphere ü§î). This was super cheap (50 PHP/0.85‚Ç¨ per hour of internet), but not as professional as I‚Äôm used to. Internet speed was really fast.

![Internet cafe Manila](/assets/img/2019-philippines/internet-cafe-manila.jpg)

The second day I went to real coworking space, Acceler8. This time the atmosphere was better but prices were a little higher (660 PHP/11.26‚Ç¨ for the day). Internet speed was fast but not as fast as in the gamer cafe. I guess people playing fortnite have higher latency standard than pro üòÇ

## A long weekend in beautiful Palawan

Then, I moved to Palawan for a long weekend. I took a 30‚Ç¨ flight to Puerto Princesa (thanks Cebu Pacific), then a 5 hour bus to El Nido.

There I did the usual snorkelling tour, spent time on the beach and went kayaking.

Finally, I took a flight to Cebu City (for 30‚Ç¨ as well), and settled there for the following 3 weeks. 

## Living in Cebu City

Cebu city is like a smaller Manila. Don‚Äôt expect to live in paradise, it‚Äôs the stereotype of the big city: lots of cars everywhere, really crowded, western fast-food everywhere, and lots of poverty. Still, the city has everything for you to be productive at work. 

![Cebu City](/assets/img/2019-philippines/cebu-city.jpg)

The first days, I just slept in a hostel (6‚Ç¨/night) and worked in a great coworking space which was amazingly cheap.

The space is named [Workplace cafe](https://www.google.com/maps/place/Workplace+Cafe/@10.3055823,123.89556,17z/data=!3m1!4b1!4m5!3m4!1s0x33a9995a9694f34d:0xb1c27bfa0541456f!8m2!3d10.305577!4d123.8977487), cost 280 PHP per day (4,78‚Ç¨/day), including a welcome drink, optic fiber, working chair and stays open until 6 AM.

I managed to find a nice apartment in a tower on Airbnb.

Big bedroom, kitchen, optic fiber, couch, TV, swimming pool and gym.

It was 18‚Ç¨/night, which is more expensive that what I‚Äôm used to in Asia (my goal is max 11‚Ç¨/night).

But here, considering the fact that it was my apartment + my working place + my gym, it removed costs that I usually pay separately, so 18‚Ç¨/night was in the budget.

![Appartment in Cebu City](/assets/img/2019-philippines/appartment.jpg)

I had a great time in this apartment, but I experienced something that I didn‚Äôt experience before: loneliness. 

In Cebu City, I didn‚Äôt find a nomad community like I experienced in Bali or Koh Lanta. No beach & beers after work, no sunset with the friends from the coworking space. The first coworking space I found was just full of Filipinos student, no nomads.

Maybe that‚Äôs just the beginning of the nomad scene in the Philippines. I know some people have been working there for years, but it‚Äôs not mainstream yet. Giving the potential of the Philippines, I‚Äôm sure it‚Äôs going to change in the coming years.
